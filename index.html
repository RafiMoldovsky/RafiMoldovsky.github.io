About me

Hello! My name is Samson Rafi Moldovsky (although I go by Rafi). I am a Computer Science and Applied Mathematics student at UCLA and plan to graduate in 2024. I chose to double major because I love to combine mathematical concepts with computer projects and software concepts. I have over a year's worth of experience working on computer science projects and learning several math topics. While my current endevers are more focused on technology, I also do have experience working in a legal office as an intern. So far, my favorite studies have been software engineering, operating system, algorithms, discrete math, and linear algebra. Moving forward, I plan to focus on my computer science interests and venture into the field of software engineering. I hope to continue to gain experience in the software field and be part of the technological innovations of the future.


Outside of my studies I love to run and workout. At UCLA, I have been part of the Spikeball team. We have practice 3 times a week and go to tournaments around the country to compete. Along with Spikeball, I love playing and watching soccer (West Ham is my team of choice). I also love spending time with my friends and family, and, while I go to school in Los Angeles, I am actually from Pennsylvania and love to visit back East.
